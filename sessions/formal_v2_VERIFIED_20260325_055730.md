**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The union of two finite subsets of natural numbers is finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Sets**: Recall that a set of natural numbers is finite if it has a cardinality that is a natural number.
2. **Union Cardinality**: The cardinality of the union of two finite sets A and B is at most the sum of their cardinalities (|A ∪ B| ≤ |A| + |B|).
3. **Finite Sum**: Since the sum of two natural numbers is also a natural number, the union's cardinality is a natural number.
4. **Conclusion**: Therefore, the union of two finite sets of natural numbers is finite.

LEAN HINT:
- Imports: `import data.nat.basic` and `import set_theory.finite`
- Tactics: `simp`, `omega`, `exact`, `apply` with lemmas like `finite.union` from Mathlib.

DIFFICULTY: easy

THEOREM: The intersection of two finite sets of natural numbers is also finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Sets**: Recall that a set of natural numbers is finite if there exists a natural number `n` such that the set is a subset of `{0, 1, 2, ..., n}`.
2. **Intersection Property**: Show that the intersection of two sets `A` and `B` is a subset of both `A` and `B`.
3. **Boundedness**: Since `A` and `B` are finite, there exist bounds `n_A` and `n_B` such that all elements of `A` are ≤ `n_A` and all elements of `B` are ≤ `n_B`. The intersection's elements are ≤ `min(n_A, n_B)`.
4. **Conclusion**: Therefore, the intersection is bounded by `min(n_A, n_B)`, making it finite.
