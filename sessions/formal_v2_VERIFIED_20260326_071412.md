**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: For any list of natural numbers, the sum of the elements of the list is equal to the sum of the elements of the list obtained by removing duplicates.

PROOF SKETCH: 
To prove this, we proceed by induction on the length of the list. For the base case, an empty list trivially satisfies the property. For the inductive step, consider a list with a duplicate element. Removing one instance of the duplicate does not change the sum, as addition is commutative and associative. Thus, by iteratively removing duplicates and applying the inductive hypothesis, we establish the result.
