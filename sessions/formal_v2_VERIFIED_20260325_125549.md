THEOREM: If a language L is in NP, then its complement (the set of all strings not in L) is in NP if and only if L is in P.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Forward Direction (⇒):** Assume the complement of L, denoted ¬L, is in NP. By the definition of NP, there exists a polynomial-time verifiable certificate for ¬L. We leverage the fact that if both L and ¬L are in NP, then L is in P. This follows from the theorem that NP ∩ co-NP ⊆ P, where co-NP is the class of complements of NP languages. Thus, L must be in P.
2. **Reverse Direction (⇐):** If L is in P, then ¬L is also in P because P is closed under complementation. Since P ⊆ NP, ¬L is in NP.
