**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The composition of a polynomial-time computable function with a polynomial-time computable function results in a polynomial-time computable function, thereby demonstrating closure under composition in the class of polynomial-time computable functions.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition Recall**: A polynomial-time computable function `f` is one where there exists a polynomial `p` such that for all inputs `x`, the computation time of `f(x)` is bounded by `p(|x|)`, where `|x|` denotes the length of `x`.
2. **Composition Definition**: Given two polynomial-time computable functions `f` and `g`, their composition `h(x) = f(g(x))` requires computing `g(x)` first, then applying `f` to the result.
3. **Time Complexity Analysis**: Let `p_f` and `p_g` be the bounding polynomials for `f` and `g`, respectively. The time to compute `g(x)` is at most `p_g(|x|)`, and the time to compute `f(g(x))` is at most `p_f(|g(x)|)`.
4. **Polynomial Bound on Composition**: Since `|g(x)|` (the length of the output of `g(x)`) is polynomially bounded in `|x|` (because `g` is polynomial-time, hence its output length is polynomial in its input length), there exists a polynomial `q` such that `p_f(|g(x)|) ≤ q(|x|)`. Thus, the total time for `h(x)` is `p_g(|x|) + q(|x|)`, which is also a polynomial in `|x|`.
5. **Conclusion**: Therefore, `h` is polynomial-time computable, proving closure under composition.

- Tactics: `apply`, `use` (for polynomial bounds), `simp` (for polynomial arithmetic), `exact` (for composing bounding polynomials)
