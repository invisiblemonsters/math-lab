THEOREM: The composition of two polynomial-time computable functions, where the output of the first function serves as the input to the second, results in a function that is also polynomial-time computable, provided that the polynomial bounds of both functions are known and composable.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definitions**: Let `f: A → B` and `g: B → C` be polynomial-time computable functions with polynomial bounds `p` and `q`, respectively.
2. **Composability**: The composition `g ∘ f` is defined as `x ↦ g(f(x))`. We need to show there exists a polynomial `r` such that the time complexity of `g ∘ f` is bounded by `r`.
3. **Polynomial Construction**: Given `p` and `q`, construct `r(x) = q(p(x))`. Since polynomial composition is closed under addition and multiplication, `r` is a valid polynomial.
4. **Time Complexity Argument**: The time taken by `g ∘ f` on input `x` is at most the time taken by `f` on `x` plus the time taken by `g` on `f(x)`, which is bounded by `p(x) + q(p(x))`. This is asymptotically dominated by `r(x) = q(p(x))`, proving polynomial-time computability of `g ∘ f`.

5. **Decider Construction**: Construct a decider `D_{L''}(x)` that computes `h(x)` and then applies `D_L` to the result.
