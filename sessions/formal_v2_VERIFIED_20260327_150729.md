**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: For any natural numbers a, b, and c, the difference of the products of a and b and a and c is equal to the product of a and the difference of b and c. Formally, a * b - a * c = a * (b - c).

PROOF SKETCH:
1. Start with the left-hand side (LHS) of the equation: a * b - a * c.
2. Factor out the common term 'a' from both products using the distributive property in reverse.
3. Show that this factorization results in a * (b - c), which matches the right-hand side (RHS).
4. Conclude that LHS = RHS by the reflexivity of equality.

- Tactics: `simp [mul_sub]` or manual proof using `rw [← mul_sub]`
