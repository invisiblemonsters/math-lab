**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The intersection of two finite sets of natural numbers is also finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition**: Recall that a set is finite if there exists a bijection with a natural number less than a certain bound.
2. **Intersection Property**: The intersection of two sets A and B, denoted A ∩ B, consists of elements common to both.
3. **Finite Intersection**: If A and B are finite, there exist natural numbers n and m such that A has n elements and B has m elements.
4. **Bounding the Intersection**: The intersection A ∩ B cannot have more elements than the smaller of A or B, specifically, |A ∩ B| ≤ min(n, m).
5. **Conclusion**: Since |A ∩ B| is bounded by a natural number, A ∩ B is finite.

  exact hB

## Turn 2 — Verified ✓

**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The union of two finite sets of natural numbers is also finite.

PROOF SKETCH: 
1. **Definition Recall**: A set of natural numbers is finite if there exists a natural number `n` such that all elements of the set are less than `n`.
2. **Union Property**: Consider two finite sets `A` and `B`. By definition, there exist natural numbers `n_A` and `n_B` such that all elements of `A` are less than `n_A` and all elements of `B` are less than `n_B`.
3. **Maximum Bound**: Let `n = max(n_A, n_B) + 1`. This ensures that all elements in both `A` and `B` are less than `n`.
4. **Conclusion**: Since all elements of `A ∪ B` are less than `n`, the union set is finite by definition.

LEAN HINT:
- Imports: `import data.set.finite`
- Tactics: `apply`, `use`, `max`, `simp`
- Key Definitions: `set.finite`, `set.union`

DIFFICULTY: easy

**Lean 4 Proof:**
```lean
import Mathlib

theorem union_of_finite_sets_is_finite (A B : Set ℕ) (hA : Set.Finite A) (hB : Set.Finite B) : 
  Set.Finite (A ∪ B) := 
Set.Finite.union hA hB
```

**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The union of a finite set of natural numbers and an infinite set of natural numbers is infinite.

PROOF SKETCH: 
1. Assume for contradiction that the union of a finite set A and an infinite set B is finite.
2. Since A is finite, the union A ∪ B being finite would imply B is also finite (as B is a subset of A ∪ B), contradicting the premise that B is infinite.
3. Therefore, the union must be infinite.

LEAN HINT:
- Imports: `import Data.Set.Finite`
- Tactics: `intro`, `contrapose`, `apply`, `exact`

DIFFICULTY: medium

**Lean 4 Proof:**

theorem finite_union_infinite {A B : Set ℕ} (hA : A.Finite) (hB : ¬ B.Finite) : ¬ (A ∪ B).Finite :=
  fun h => hB (Set.Finite.subset (Set.subset_union_right A B) h)
