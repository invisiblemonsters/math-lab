**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The intersection of two finite sets of natural numbers is also finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition**: Recall that a set is finite if there exists a bijection with a natural number less than a certain bound.
2. **Intersection Property**: The intersection of two sets A and B, denoted A ∩ B, consists of elements common to both.
3. **Finite Intersection**: If A and B are finite, there exist natural numbers n and m such that A has n elements and B has m elements.
4. **Bounding the Intersection**: The intersection A ∩ B cannot have more elements than the smaller of A or B, specifically, |A ∩ B| ≤ min(n, m).
5. **Conclusion**: Since |A ∩ B| is bounded by a natural number, A ∩ B is finite.
