THEOREM: The sum of the lengths of two lists is equal to the length of their concatenation.

PROOF SKETCH: 
1. Consider two arbitrary lists `l1` and `l2`.
2. By definition, the length of a list is the number of elements in it.
3. The concatenation of `l1` and `l2`, denoted `l1 ++ l2`, appends all elements of `l2` to the end of `l1`.
4. The length of `l1 ++ l2` is the total count of elements from both `l1` and `l2`.
5. Thus, `length(l1) + length(l2) = length(l1 ++ l2)` by basic arithmetic and the definition of list concatenation.

LEAN HINT:
```lean
import Data.List.Basic

theorem length_concat_eq_sum (l1 l2 : List α) : length l1 + length l2 = length (l1 ++ l2) := by
  induction l1; simp [*, Nat.add_comm]
```
