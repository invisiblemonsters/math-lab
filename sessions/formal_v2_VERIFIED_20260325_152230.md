THEOREM: The sum of the first n natural numbers, denoted as 1 + 2 + ... + n, is equal to n(n + 1)/2. This formula provides a closed-form expression for the sum of an arithmetic series with common difference 1.

PROOF SKETCH: We will use mathematical induction to prove this statement. 
1. **Base Case**: Verify the formula for n = 1. 
2. **Inductive Step**: Assume the formula holds for some arbitrary natural number k (i.e., 1 + 2 + ... + k = k(k + 1)/2). Then, show that it also holds for k + 1 by adding (k + 1) to both sides of the inductive hypothesis and simplifying to match the desired formula.
