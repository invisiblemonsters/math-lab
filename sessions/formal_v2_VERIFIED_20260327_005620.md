**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The intersection of two finite sets of natural numbers is also finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Set**: Recall that a set is finite if there exists a natural number n such that the set has exactly n elements.
2. **Intersection Property**: Consider two finite sets A and B. The intersection A ∩ B consists of elements common to both A and B.
3. **Cardinality Argument**: The number of elements in A ∩ B cannot exceed the number of elements in either A or B. Specifically, |A ∩ B| ≤ min(|A|, |B|).
4. **Finite Cardinality**: Since both |A| and |B| are finite, min(|A|, |B|) is also finite. Hence, A ∩ B has a finite number of elements.
5. **Conclusion**: Therefore, the intersection of two finite sets of natural numbers is finite.

- Tactics: `apply`, `exact`, `omega` (for cardinality arguments)
- Suggested Structure:

  -- apply definition of finite, use cardinality properties

THEOREM: The union of two finite sets of natural numbers is also finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Set**: Recall that a set is finite if it has a cardinality equal to some natural number.
2. **Union Cardinality**: Use the property that the cardinality of the union of two sets is less than or equal to the sum of their cardinalities (i.e., |A ∪ B| ≤ |A| + |B|).
3. **Finite Sum**: Since the sum of two natural numbers is also a natural number, the union's cardinality is bounded by a natural number.
4. **Conclusion**: Therefore, the union of two finite sets of natural numbers is finite.

LEAN HINT:
- Imports: `import Data.Nat.Basic`, `import Data.Set.Finite`
- Tactics: `apply`, `simp`, `omega` (for natural number arithmetic)

DIFFICULTY: easy
