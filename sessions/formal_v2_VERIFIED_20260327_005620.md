**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The intersection of two finite sets of natural numbers is also finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Set**: Recall that a set is finite if there exists a natural number n such that the set has exactly n elements.
2. **Intersection Property**: Consider two finite sets A and B. The intersection A ∩ B consists of elements common to both A and B.
3. **Cardinality Argument**: The number of elements in A ∩ B cannot exceed the number of elements in either A or B. Specifically, |A ∩ B| ≤ min(|A|, |B|).
4. **Finite Cardinality**: Since both |A| and |B| are finite, min(|A|, |B|) is also finite. Hence, A ∩ B has a finite number of elements.
5. **Conclusion**: Therefore, the intersection of two finite sets of natural numbers is finite.
