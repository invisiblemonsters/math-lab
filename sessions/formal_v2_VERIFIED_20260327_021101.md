PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Set**: Recall that a set is finite if it has a cardinality less than some natural number (i.e., there exists a bijection with a natural number less than n).

THEOREM: The composition of a polynomial function with itself results in a polynomial function. Formally, if f : ℕ → ℕ is a polynomial function, then the function g : ℕ → ℕ defined by g(n) = f(f(n)) is also a polynomial function.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition Recall**: A polynomial function f(n) can be expressed as a sum of terms of the form a_k * n^k, where a_k are coefficients and k is a non-negative integer.
2. **Composition Definition**: Substitute f(n) into itself, yielding g(n) = f(f(n)).
3. **Expand Composition**: Substitute the polynomial expression for f(n) into itself, resulting in a nested polynomial.
4. **Distribute and Combine**: Expand the nested polynomial, distributing coefficients and combining like terms. This process will terminate because the degree of the resulting polynomial will be finite (specifically, the degree of g(n) will be the square of the degree of f(n)).
5. **Conclusion**: The expanded form of g(n) will be a polynomial in n, thus g is a polynomial function.
