**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The union of a finite set of natural numbers with a singleton set is finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Set**: Recall that a set is finite if it has a cardinality less than some natural number (i.e., there exists a bijection with a natural number less than n).
2. **Union with Singleton**: Consider a finite set A and a singleton set {a}. The union A ∪ {a} can have at most |A| + 1 elements.
3. **Cardinality Argument**: Since |A| is finite, |A| + 1 is also finite. Thus, the union A ∪ {a} is finite.

**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The intersection of two finite sets of natural numbers is also finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Set**: Recall that a set is finite if there exists a bijection with some initial segment of the natural numbers (i.e., {0, 1, ..., n-1} for some n).
2. **Intersection Property**: Show that the intersection of two sets A and B, denoted A ∩ B, is a subset of both A and B.
3. **Subset of Finite Set**: Prove that any subset of a finite set is also finite. This can be done by showing that a subset of {0, 1, ..., n-1} can be injectively mapped into {0, 1, ..., n-1} itself, preserving finiteness.
4. **Combine Results**: Since A ∩ B is a subset of A (which is finite), it follows that A ∩ B is finite.

LEAN HINT:
- Imports: `import data.set.finite`
- Tactics: `intro`, `apply`, `exact`, `simp`, `use` (for constructing injections)

DIFFICULTY: easy
