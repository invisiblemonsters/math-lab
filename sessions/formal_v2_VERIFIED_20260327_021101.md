**Natural Language:**
THEOREM: The union of a finite set of natural numbers with a singleton set is finite.

PROOF SKETCH:
1. **Definition of Finite Set**: Recall that a set is finite if it has a cardinality less than some natural number (i.e., there exists a bijection with a natural number less than n).
2. **Union with Singleton**: Consider a finite set A and a singleton set {a}. The union A ∪ {a} can have at most |A| + 1 elements.
3. **Cardinality Argument**: Since |A| is finite, |A| + 1 is also finite. Thus, the union A ∪ {a} is finite.
